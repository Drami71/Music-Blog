La Dispute-Panorama

Released March 22, 2019

Ruminating on topics such as grief, loss, and death La Dispute take us on a journey. Seamlessly blending guitar, bass, and drums no one instrument takes center stage. Each bleed off the other and create a soft, moody sound. Dreyer comes out swinging and delivers a performance both vocally and lyrically. Always employing his style of spoken word, none can do the remarkable job that he did in displaying such a level of emotion and vulnerability.

American Football-American Football(LP3)

Released March 22, 2019

This third album takes the Midwestern emo band into a slightly new direction. Exploring new concepts as each member is exploring newfound fatherhood, they decide to use aging as a way to conceal the trials and tribulations as something that can be only understood as genetic. Few bands can do what American Football has done for the better part of twenty years: effortlessly bleed in shoegaze and post-rock into emo to create an album that feeds off vibes more than feelings.

PUP-Morbid Stuff

Released April 5, 2019

PUP continue their charm the third time around, creating an album containing all that we’ve fallen in with over the years: nasally vocals, sarcastic and scathing lyrics and glorious punk riffs. With more vulnerable lyrics that we’ve never seen from Babcock, there’s no better example of a classic modern punk record than what PUP have been able to achieve. Keeping their iconic sound while showing signs of progressive growth, one can only think on what's to come next.

Frank Carter & the Rattlesnakes-End of Suffering

Released May 3, 2019

Shedding off their hardcore roots, we see Frank Carter become a juggernaut rock band. Frank Carter has never shyed away from crafting songs with blunt but honest lyrics, tackling his personal demons. Highlights from the album include Tyrant Lizard King which has the old Frank Carter swag and Love Games which has a monster riff that's too good to miss.

Thank You Scientist-Terraformer

Released June 14, 2019

Sitting at 84 minutes, this album can be hard to digest at first with all the moving pieces from the seven-piece band. With longer song lengths, there is less time to build on each one and instead helps to showcase different styles within each song. All the chaotic moments might be too difficult for some but figuring out and dissecting each song is a worthwhile ride in one of the best albums of the year.
