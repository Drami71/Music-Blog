2020 In Hardcore

Keep a lookout for Nail's new album releasing 2nd half of this year.

Higher Power-27 Miles Underwater

Released January 24, 2020

The second album out of the English hardcore band treads familiar ground with much of their contemporaries, but that's not quite a bad thing. It's a wonderful mix of hardcore, grunge and 90s indiie rock, which sounds like a weird mix but in the end it works. From the musicianship to Jimmy Wizards wonderfully eclectic vocal performance, this album will surely be a mainstay for the entire year.

Envy-The Fallen Crimson

Released February 5, 2020

Having been around the hardcore scene for almost 30 years, Envy are no strangers. They've been masters at mixing the harshness of hardcore with post-rock for decades. This album sounds overwhelming at points jumping from section to section. The album itself feels like a tide is washing over you and at this point in their careers it's something we've come to expect.

Code Orange-Underneath

Released March 13, 2020

This time around, Code Orange continue fusing their metallic and melodic sides with electronic elements. Introducing a more industrial synthetic sound mixed with their usual chaotic, often mathematical approach, Code Orange has paved a new road. Each track cleanly flows off each other, going from a heavy to a more melodic track. It can be a little in your face at times but at the end of the day this is a hardcore album after all.

The Chats-High Risk Behaviour

Released March 27, 2020

The Chats pull off something most bands can't: creating a carefree, sardonic record that sounds so natural and real. The Chats are a band that always focuses on having a good time, often in a juvenile way. Tracks don't run past the two-minute mark which helps to keep a steady flow. The Chats don't care about breaking down barriers, they just want to have fun.
