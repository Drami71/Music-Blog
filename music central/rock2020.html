2020 In Rock

Keep a lookout for the new The Used album releasing later this year.

Nine Inch Nails-Ghosts V: Together/Ghosts VI: Locusts

Released March 26, 2020

Parts 5 and 6 of the Ghosts saga were released simultaneously, offering a clear contrast between the two. Both volumes feel more concentrated compared to the original quartet released back in 2008. Together has a more hopeful, often comforting sound. Locuston the other hand has a more dread, anxiety inducing sound. Emblamatic of the time that these two albums were released, they feel right at home.

Waxahatchee-Saint Cloud

Released March 27, 2020

The new album is often not one you're expecting. The imagery used evokes one of warmness yet it is an album speaking of one's suffering and trying to overcome addiction. One who is trying to better themselves, something that will happen numerous times in our lifetimes. It's an album that embraces just how difficult and messy growing up and taking responsibility for one's own actions can be.

The Strokes-The New Abnormal

Released April 10, 2020

Their first album in seven years and arguably their best in 15 years, The Strokes have found their old groove again. Using their old techniques of incorporating 80's synthpop, they tap into an old nostalgia. This album isn't revolutionary but its an enjoyable album with catchy tunes and a return to form for The Strokes.

Enter Shikari-Nothing Is True & Everything Is Possible

Released April 17, 2020

Nothing Is True & Everything Is Permitted feels like a natural endpoint for Enter Shikari. Their genre-hopping sound feels like it's come full-circle and pays tribute to their entire discography. Ranging from the synthpop to their punk-rock days, it's an album of ideas and experimentation that's more ambitious than anything they have ever attempted.
