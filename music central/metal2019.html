Slipknot-We Are Not Your Kind

Released August 9, 2019

We Are Not Your Kind feels like the album Slipknot has been trying to make since Vol. 3. From the gutteral guitars to the vocals to the use of synthesizers to bring out the somber atmosphere, Slipknot has crafted an intense listen from start to end. One can't help out but single out Corey Taylor's incredible performance, both vocally and lyrically. Finally shedding off the notion that they can't replicate their first two albums, Slipknot has created a new identity that will be exciting to see where they go next.

Tool-Fear Inoculum

Released August 30, 2019

Tool’s long-awaited new album turns out to be well worth the wait. While there are hints of their older writing, the band has evolved with the instrumentalists laying down some of their most impressive and technical performances. From the guitar melody in Invincible to the vocals on Descending, Tool hasn’t lost a step after all these years. This album rewards patience. Each track is multilayered and at times feels like they don't follow the traditional structure for a song. But then again, this is Tool we are talking about so it should come as no surprise.

Cult of Luna-Dawn to Fear

Released September 20, 2019

On their eighth studio album, Cult of Luna tread familiar ground but at the same take a different shape. After Julie Christmas’s collaboration on their previous album Mariner, Cult of Luna had a lot to live up to. This time around, they created a slower, moodier album leaves them more space to work on more hypnotic riffs, twisting them into different forms until there is nothing left. A dense album that demands the attention of the listener, the runtime of nearly 80 minutes leaves you feeling like you went on a journey into the vulernabilities of mankind.

Opeth-In Caude Venenum

Released September 27, 2019

Continuing down a different, often controversial path from their original sound, Opeth keeps following their new path incorporating a more folkish progressive rock to their previous heavy metal upbringings. Creating an album that incorporates some of their old sound, their are some more heavier moments interwoven among the slower, more melodic melodies. Releasing two different versions with English and Swedish vocals, one may be tempted to just listen to the English version. However, their is something about how the Swedish vocals feeds off the instrumentaion that warrants a listen.

Blood Incantation-Hidden History of the Human Race

Released November 11, 2019

On their second full length album, Blood Incantation release quite arguably the decades defining death metal album. Creating such a psychedelic trip that runs at just 36 minutes across 4 tracks, it wastes no time in creating a weird atmosphere with riffs that spans the entirety of death metal. Not only paying homage to old death metal bands like Death and Cannibal Corpse, they incorporate a more modern, progressive sound found in a band like Mastodon. In the end, they are able to morph these two styles into something truly special, quite arguably not just the best death metal album of the year but of the decade.
