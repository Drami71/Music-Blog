Angel Dust-Pretty Buff

Released March 15, 2019

Listening to Pretty Buff for the first time might leave you scratching your head given Angel Dust's first two albums. While not quick as fast-paced punk rock that we are accustomed to, Pretty Buff shows a evolution for the band. Songs are allowed to breathe more and tracks feel more connected than before. There's nothing bombastic or grandeous anout the album- just 13 great, easy to listen songs, each crafted with such honesty and care.

Knocked Loose-A Different Shade of Blue

Released August 23, 2019

After announcing their arrival with their fantastic first LP, Knocked Loose showed up with no restraints. On their explosive second LP, we see a more mature and focused band that has found their groove. An unrelenting album from beginning to end, its fast and heavy. With bigger riffs, meaty breakdowns and more fleshed out lyrics Knocked Loose shows that they’ll be a mainstay for years to come.

SeeYouSpaceCowboy-The Correlation Between Entrance and Exit Wounds

Released September 27, 2019

On their proper debut LP, we see a band that is no longer just a breakdown frenzy writing squad. We see a growth in their songwriting highlighted through the instrumentation and lyrics. With songs that deal with survival in a world filled with constant oppoisition, loss and tragedy many other hardcore bands can't pull off this level of emotion and maturity this early in their careers.

Norma Jean-All Hail

Released October 25, 2019

There's not much that can really be said about Norma Jean at this point. They are running like a well-oiled machine. After all these years they show why they are still atop the metalcore world. Everything is on display here: disgusting breakdowns, time signature switches galore and beautiful melodies. All Hail feels like the culmination of all the years of hard work since Meridional and its paid off.

Counterparts-Nothing Left to Love

Released November 1, 2019

Considered to be at the forefront of the new wave of hardcore, Counterparts have attained a mass of loyal followers over the years. They are at this part in their career where it feels like they’ve reached their final form. All the different iterations of their sound over the years feels like they are equally represented and refined upon. Between the melodic breakdowns and Murphy’s tortured lyrics, Counterparts puts out another quality record.
