2020 In Metal

Sepultura-Quadra

Released February 7, 2020

Sepultura releases arguably their best album in decades. Quadra feels like their first album that stands toe to toe with their original trilogy. The orchestras at place in the album help to counterbalance the heaviness we've come to expect. Sepultura are back and better than ever.

Psychotic Waltz-The God-Shaped Void

Released February 14, 2020

The first album in almost 24 years is here and shows how the band hasn't lost any steam. Arguably one of the best prog metal bands of the 90's, their new album show a slight evolution of their sound without losing any of the eccentric takes on metal that made them so prominent. Heavy riffing, aggressive vocals and complex time signatures, this album will be at the forefront of the conversation in the prog-metal community.

Old Man Gloom-Seminar IX:Darkness of Being

Released March 23, 2020

On a surprise digital release, Old Man Gloom come out swinging with a emotional album as a tribute for their fallen friend. Branching off their previous records, Old Man Gloom make a record with incredible melodies that serve as a great contrast with the ensuing heavy riffs. Seminar IX feels like their most complete record to date and an excellent addition to the near flawless discography.

Igorr- Spirituality and Distortion

Released March 27, 2020

Igorr has never shyed away from creating avant-garde music with such flair. With an increased focus on songwriting this time around, Igorr have managed to create a compelling album from start to finish with all the lunacy that is already expected. From baroque styles to electronic influences to thrashing metal, it's certainly a journey one won't forget. Not an easy listen, but one that is worthwhile for those willing to take the plunge.
